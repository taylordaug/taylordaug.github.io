Why Does Everyone Hate JavaScript?

February 11, 2016

I was apprehensive about getting going with JavaScript this week. Here's what I was hearing:

"JavaScript is a miserable programming language. Anyone who tells you differently has advanced JavaScript stockholm syndrome."

"JavaScript goes to such enormous lengths to avoid crashing that it impedes its usability."

"It sucks."

The fact is, JavaScript isn't going anywhere. By understanding what people hate so much about it, I'll know what to look out for when I'm running into issues. The main thing that I've encountered is JavaScript's accepting of everything. This lack of feedback and incoherent error messaging makes it tough to pinpoint what is going wrong.
